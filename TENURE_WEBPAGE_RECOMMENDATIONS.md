# Tenure-Facing Webpage Recommendations

This draft updates the public site to read less like a compact CV and more like an independent research profile. It now uses separate pages rather than one long scrolling document. The changes are intentionally factual and conservative; anything requiring missing information is listed below instead of being published as a placeholder.

## Changes already drafted

- Use the new portrait as the main profile image and add descriptive alt text.
- Include the TUM logo as a subtle institutional mark in the navigation, homepage, and footer.
- Replace the Cornell favicon with a neutral personal `MD` favicon.
- Update the title and metadata to foreground Mathematical Statistics and Data Science at TUM.
- Replace the long scrolling page with separate pages: `index.html`, `research.html`, `publications.html`, `teaching.html`, `talks.html`, `students.html`, and `contact.html`.
- Fix the broken `CV` navigation link by linking directly to `cv.pdf` from every page.
- Add a dedicated `Research` page that frames an independent research program.
- Keep `publications.html` as a complete, direct publication list without a selected-publications block.
- Add `Teaching and Mentoring` framing, while keeping the existing course list.
- Add a `Students` page to support recruitment and mentoring visibility.
- Modernize the visual hierarchy with top navigation, a TUM-aligned blue palette, research tags, compact focus panels, cleaner publication lists, and a less dated homepage portrait treatment.

## High-priority additions before using this for tenure materials

- Update `cv.pdf` so the first page clearly lists the TUM position, appointment date, research areas, education, selected honors, grants, students, service, and invited talks.
- Add a short `Funding and Grants` section once you have items to list. Suggested format: grant title, role, funder, years, amount if public, and collaborators.
- Add a `Students and Mentoring` section when you have supervised students, thesis projects, research assistants, or reading groups to list.
- Add a `Service` section with departmental service, seminar organization, conference organization, editorial work, refereeing, and professional society service.
- Add `Software, Data, and Reproducibility` if you have packages, code repositories, reproducible supplements, or datasets connected to your papers.
- Add a small `News` area only if you can keep it current. Good items are accepted papers, grants, new preprints, invited talks, and student milestones.

## Suggested public wording for missing sections

### Funding and Grants

Use this only after replacing the bracketed text:

> My research is supported by [funder/program]. Current and recent projects include [project title], where I serve as [PI/co-PI/collaborator], focusing on [one-sentence goal].

### Academic Service

Use this only after replacing the bracketed text:

> I contribute to the statistics and probability communities through [journal/conference refereeing], [seminar or workshop organization], and [departmental or university service].

### Students and Mentoring

Use this only after replacing the bracketed text:

> I currently supervise [PhD/MSc/BSc/research] students working on [topics]. I am especially interested in mentoring students who want to combine probability theory, statistical methodology, and applications to dependent data.

## Style notes

- Keep the homepage focused on identity, role, research area, and links. Avoid a long chronological biography at the very top.
- Lead with independent research themes before the publication inventory.
- Keep `Publications` complete and easy to scan. If you prefer not to single out selected work, the page can stay as a direct full list.
- Avoid mentioning tenure directly on the public site. The page should communicate trajectory, independence, visibility, teaching, mentoring, and service without sounding procedural.
